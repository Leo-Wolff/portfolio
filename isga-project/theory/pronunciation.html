Pronunciation

Dutch is a very phonetic language. Pronouncing words in Dutch thus solely depends on understanding which sound is connected to which letters.

Note: everything marked with a * is a sound that doesn’t exist in the English language.

Dutch sound English comparison
a "Start"
aa "California"
aai/oei/ooi Combine aa/oe/oo with ie
b Same in English
ch/g* Clear your throat
d Same in English
e "Net"
ee "Gate"
eeuw/ieuw/uw Combine ee/ie/uu with oe
ei/ij "Fight"
eu* "Hurt" but with lips rounded
f Same in English
h Same in English
i Same in English
ie "Read"
j "Yes"
l Same in English
m Same in English
n Same in English
o* "Paw" but shorter
oe "Book"
oo "Go"
ou/au "Now"
r* "Really" but with more roll
s Same in English
u "Alive"
ui Combine a and uu
uu* "Ew" but with lips rounded
v "Fairy"
w "Very"
z Same in English
