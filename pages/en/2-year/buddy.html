Book Buddy

Process

  • In this assignment I immediately wanted to take inspiration of an activity that I myself do frequently: reading in the train. I adore reading, but I can never find the time for it. In 2021 I had the plan to start reading again and I found that reading whilst traveling to school was the perfect moment to make time for it.

  • For the multi-device experience it was mostly about switching between different reading media. Audiobooks, e-books, but also physical books are all the things I wanted to connect together via the app. Mostly designing for physical books was a difficult challenge, how can an app know where you are in a book if the book itself isn’t digital? After tons of struggling and collecting inspiration I came up with a small form idea; indicate which page you are on and what your last read sentence was. The user may type as much of the sentence as they like; the more the better. The app can go through the entire book and exactly know where they are on the page.

  • Afterwards the designing of the wireflow and then the upgrade to a screenflow. With the wireframes I worked in detail on purpose, thus the screenflow process was incredibly fast and easy.

Assignment

Think of and design a multi-device experience for a certain activity. Visualise your solution in a screenflow so that the functionality of your product across all used devices is completely shown.
